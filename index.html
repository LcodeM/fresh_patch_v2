Benefits of growing your own food


We love gardening for so many reasons, but here are a few that we think can benefit everyone:


  • Become self-sufficient and save your pennies by sourcing your own food
  • Find peace and serenity through nurturing your space
  • Develop and maintain healthier lifestyle choices
  • Create and teach good habits for your family and loved ones
  • Reconnect with nature and the outdoors

Top Tips for Beginners


Here's a few useful considerations to get you right on track for starting your very own vegetable patch:


  1. Get to know your space
  2. Plan you patch
  3. Start small (and easy!)
  4. Get rid of any weeds in the area
  5. Make sure to care for your patch regularly

Today's Top Tip


Discover the average first and last frost dates for your area.

Planting too early (or late) in the season can spell disaster for your garden.

You need to know the last average spring frost date for your area so you don't accidentally kill plants by putting them out prematurely.

It's also good to know your first average fall frost date so that you get your plants harvested or moved indoors before late-season cold damages them.
